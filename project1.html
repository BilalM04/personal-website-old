Summary

Design Scenario:

As part of an engineering design team in Project One, we focused on designing a wind turbine blade for Calgary homeowners. Our assignment, the Roof Generator scenario, required us to create a blade that would be suitable for installation on a residential roof and generate electricity for the building.

Project Tasks:

  • Conduct research on wind turbine specifications.
  • Determine the necessary requirements for the turbine blade.
  • Select materials based on their mechanical properties and performance.
  • Use computer-aided design (CAD) tools to model the blade.
  • Ensure the blade can withstand the required design load through virtual simulations.

Skills

Skills Applied in Project 1:

🛠  Gained a deeper understanding of engineering concepts and material selection.

📈 Used MPIs (Material Property Indices) to aid in the material selection process.

💡 Used decision matrices to arrive at a final material selection.

💻 Used Autodesk Inventor to run simulations and evaluate the stiffness and deflection of turbine blade.

👥 Developed teamwork skills and gained an understanding of the importance of good design.

  • Used Granta Edupack to plot our MPI and perform a material selection.

  • Used Autodesk Inventor to test the deflection of our turbine blade and alter its thickness.

Learning:

During project 1, I gained a deeper understanding of engineering concepts and material selection methods. Specifically, I learned how to use MPIs (Material Property Indices) and decision matrices to determine the most suitable material for our turbine blade. I also had the opportunity to use Autodesk to run simulations and evaluate the stiffness and deflection of the blade. In addition to technical skills, this project allowed me to develop my teamwork skills and understand the importance of good design. Overall, I feel that project 1 was a valuable learning experience that has equipped me with a strong foundation in these areas.
